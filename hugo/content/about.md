I'm a software developer based in Warsaw, Poland, with a degree from the [Warsaw University of Technology](http://www.elka.pw.edu.pl/eng) (Faculty of Electronics and Information Technology).

I've been building mobile applications professionally since 2010 — starting with iOS, and expanding into cross-platform mobile over the past eight years. First via Java with j2objc, now with **Kotlin Multiplatform (KMP)**, sharing business logic between iOS and Android. Swift is still my home, but the shared layer is where a lot of the interesting architecture decisions happen.

### Current role

I'm a **Tech Lead at [Beekeeper](https://www.beekeeper.io)**, leading a team of nine engineers. We build products for frontline workers — the people running shifts, managing crews, and working without a desk. We operate in a post-merger environment, which means navigating a lot of complexity beyond just the technical kind.

### Career

My first commercial project was **Polityka on iPad** (2010) — the Polish weekly magazine's app, one of the first in Poland to use auto-renewable subscriptions.

In 2013 I moved to Berlin and joined **Neofonie Mobile GmbH**, where I worked on two projects I'm still proud of: the **Bild on iPad** app for Germany's largest newspaper, and the **Digital Concert Hall** for the Berliner Philharmoniker.

Back in Warsaw, I built enterprise software for US clients at **Taptera Poland**, then led the iOS team at **Applause Inc.** (2014–2017), focusing on delivery processes and team workflows.

A consulting period followed — working on **Crescendo** at Sependa and leading an iOS team at **YND Consult** — before I spent four years (2018–2022) at the Paris-based startup [**The Fabulous**](https://thefabulous.co). It's a science-based habit-building app incubated in Duke University's Behavioral Economics Lab, and one of the more technically and humanly interesting things I've worked on.

In October 2022 I joined **Beekeeper**, initially as Principal Mobile Engineer and now as Tech Lead.

### Also

I co-founded **Mobile Academy**, an organization that ran hands-on mobile development workshops across Poland and Europe — covering TDD, architecture, and whatever was hard to get right at the time.
